📋 Cadre de l'épreuve de contrôle

Anglais · E2 et E3 · BTS Professions Immobilières · Arrêté du 22 novembre 2023 mod. 24 juin 2025

📌 Ce que l'examinateur attend
Comprendre l'essentiel d'un document (écrit ou image). S'exprimer clairement en anglais. Donner un avis structuré. Utiliser du vocabulaire immobilier de base. L'examinateur n'attend pas un anglais parfait — il évalue la communication.
1. Format de l'épreuve
DuréePréparation : 20 minutes
Présentation orale : 20 minutes
Coeff. E2 : 2 · Coeff. E3 : 2
Support remis1 à 2 documents sur un thème lié à l'immobilier ou à la société. Texte, image, ou document mixte. Parfois une annonce immobilière ou un e-mail professionnel.
DéroulementPrésentation libre du document (10 min environ). Questions de l'examinateur en anglais (10 min). Possibilité de jeu de rôle ou situation professionnelle simulée.
Niveau viséB1–B2 du CECRL. En pratique : communiquer efficacement prime sur la grammaire. Un candidat qui parle simplement mais clairement valide.
2. Types de documents
Written documentsNews article Email Property listing Report extract Website page Testimonial
Visual documentsPhotograph Advertisement Infographic Graph / chart Property photo
Professional documentsClient email Rental listing Property description Market report
3. Thématiques fréquentes

✗
Erreurs à éviter absolument : traduire mot à mot · rester silencieux · répondre uniquement « yes » ou « no » · chercher la perfection grammaticale · avoir peur des fautes · parler en français.

🔧 Les 4 méthodes essentielles

Outils à appliquer pendant les 20 minutes de préparation et pendant l'oral.

🕐 Gérer les 20 minutes de préparation
5'
Read / Observe : lire ou observer le document. Souligner les mots-clés. Identifier le type de document.
10'
Prepare : appliquer QQWWH. Identifier 3 idées principales. Chercher 2 exemples. Préparer son opinion avec PEE.
5'
Structure : rédiger sa phrase d'introduction. Préparer la conclusion. Anticiper les questions de l'examinateur.
Method 1 — Understand : QQWWH
Who?Who is speaking? Who is the audience? Qui parle ? À qui ?
What?What is the topic? What is the main message? Quel est le sujet ?
Where? When?What is the context? When was it published? Contexte géographique et temporel.
Why? How?Why was it written? What angle? Intention de l'auteur.
💡
Answer these 6 questions mentally in 2 minutes. They structure your introduction.
Method 2 — Analyse : CCCS
CausesWhy does this situation exist? Pourquoi cette situation existe-t-elle ?
ConsequencesWhat are the effects? Quels sont les effets ?
ComparisonsHow does it compare? Better or worse than before? Comparaisons utiles.
SolutionsWhat solutions are proposed? Quelles réponses possibles ?
💡
CCCS works for almost every topic. It structures the development of your presentation.
Method 3 — Analyse an image : DIC
DescribeWhat can you see? Describe the people, objects, colours, composition. Décrire les éléments visibles.
InterpretWhat does the image mean? What message does the author want to convey? Interpréter le message.
CommentWhat do you think about it? Link to current events, your own knowledge. Donner un avis élargi.
✗
Common mistake : only describing the image without interpreting it. The examiner expects a message to be identified, not just "I can see a man and a building."
Method 4 — Give your opinion : PEE
PointState your opinion clearly. « I think… / In my opinion… / I believe… »
ExplainGive a reason. « Because… / This is because… / The reason is… »
ExampleSupport with a concrete example. « For example… / For instance… »
PEE — exemple appliqué · Housing crisis
P
I think the housing crisis is one of the biggest challenges for young people today.
E
Because rents have increased much faster than salaries, making it very difficult to find an affordable flat in big cities.
E
For example, in Paris or Lyon, young workers often spend more than 40% of their income on rent, which leaves very little for other expenses.
📐 Plan oral universel
1
Introduction : present the document (type, source, topic). State the main idea in one sentence. Announce your plan briefly.
2
Development : causes / consequences / solutions (CCCS). Use linking words. Add a personal example or opinion.
3
Conclusion : summarise in one sentence. Open with a broader question or personal view. Do not introduce new ideas.

🗣 Examples — présentations orales modèles

3 documents fictifs traités avec une présentation orale en anglais. À lire à voix haute.

How to use these examples : read the document, prepare your own presentation (20 min), then reveal the model. Compare your approach with the suggested answer.

📰 Example 1 — Article · Housing crisis & young people

Fictitious document · Written text · Real estate theme

Document : "Young people priced out of the housing market"
Fictitious article · Real Estate Weekly · March 2024
According to a recent survey, more than 60% of people under 30 in major European cities spend over 40% of their income on rent. Rising house prices and stagnant wages mean that buying a property has become almost impossible for young adults without family support. Experts warn that this trend could have serious long-term consequences on social mobility and inequality. Some governments are considering new policies such as rent controls and affordable housing programmes to address the issue.
Oral presentation model — 4 to 5 minutes
Introduction
"This document is a news article from Real Estate Weekly, published in March 2024. It deals with the housing crisis and its impact on young people in European cities. The main idea is that rising rents and house prices are making it extremely difficult for young adults to find affordable housing. I will first present the main problem, then explain its causes and consequences, and finally give my opinion."
Development — Causes & Consequences
"The article shows that more than 60% of young people spend over 40% of their income on rent. This is because house prices have increased much faster than salaries. As a result, buying a flat is almost impossible without financial help from family. This could increase social inequality in the long term, because young people from less wealthy families are the most affected."
Opinion + Example
"In my opinion, this is one of the most important challenges for society today. Governments must act — for example, by building more social housing or introducing rent controls, as some cities like Berlin or Barcelona have tried. Without action, the gap between rich and poor will continue to grow."
Conclusion
"To conclude, this article shows that the housing crisis is not just an economic problem — it is also a social one. It raises the question: what kind of society do we want, where access to housing depends on your family's wealth?"

🖼 Example 2 — Image · Remote working

Fictitious document · Visual · Society theme

Document : Photograph — a person working from home
Fictitious photo · Context: remote working debate · 2024
A person is sitting at a desk at home, laptop open, surrounded by plants and natural light. The background shows a cosy living room. The person looks relaxed and focused. A caption reads: "Work from home — the new normal?"
Oral presentation model — DIC method
Describe
"In this photograph, I can see a person working from home. They are sitting at a desk with a laptop, surrounded by plants and natural light. The atmosphere looks calm and comfortable. There is a caption that says 'Work from home — the new normal?'"
Interpret
"The picture represents the rise of remote working, which has become very common since the pandemic in 2020. The caption asks whether this is now the 'new normal', suggesting that working from home is no longer exceptional but part of everyday life. The image seems to present remote working in a positive way — the person looks happy and productive."
Comment
"In my opinion, remote working has both advantages and disadvantages. The main advantage is flexibility — people save time on commuting and can have a better work-life balance. However, it can also be isolating, and not everyone has a comfortable space to work at home. For a real estate agent, for example, some tasks can be done remotely, but meeting clients requires physical presence. I think a mix of office and home working is probably the best solution."

📊 Example 3 — Graph · Energy renovation

Fictitious document · Chart · Real estate / environment theme

Document : Bar chart — "Energy performance of housing in France (2024)"
Fictitious source · Ministry of Housing · 2024
The bar chart shows the percentage of homes in each energy category (A to G). Category A: 3%. B: 8%. C: 15%. D: 22%. E: 20%. F: 17%. G: 15%. Title: "Over 30% of French homes are energy-inefficient (F or G)."
Oral presentation model — chart analysis
Present the document
"This document is a bar chart from the French Ministry of Housing, published in 2024. It shows the energy performance of homes in France. The main message is that more than 30% of French homes are in the worst energy categories — F or G — meaning they are very inefficient and expensive to heat."
Interpret — do not just read the numbers
"I will not just read the figures — what this chart tells us is that France faces a major challenge: a large proportion of its housing stock wastes energy. This is important because in 2023, French law started banning the rental of G-rated properties, and F-rated properties will follow. This means many landlords must renovate or sell their properties."
Opinion + Conclusion
"In my opinion, energy renovation is essential for both the environment and for tenants who pay high energy bills. However, it is expensive, and many owners cannot afford it. The question is: who will pay for the renovation — the landlord, the tenant, or the government? This is one of the key debates in the French housing market today."

🧠 Memo final — à relire la veille et le matin

12 réflexes · Phrases-clés · Erreurs interdites

✅
Si vous ne retenez que trois choses : 1) ne jamais rester silencieux. 2) structurer avec QQWWH + PEE. 3) utiliser les linking words (first, then, however, finally).
12 réflexes essentiels
1 — QQWWH firstIdentify Who, What, Where, When, Why, How before speaking. It structures your introduction.
2 — One sentence = one ideaKeep sentences short and clear. Better simple and correct than long and confused.
3 — CCCS for developmentCauses → Consequences → Comparisons → Solutions. Works for any topic.
4 — DIC for imagesDescribe → Interpret → Comment. Never just describe. The examiner wants the message.
5 — PEE for opinionsPoint → Explain → Example. Never say "I think" without explaining why and giving an example.
6 — Linking wordsFirst / Then / Moreover / However / Finally / As a result / On the other hand.
7 — Real estate vocabularyProperty, landlord, tenant, rent, lease, mortgage, housing crisis, energy renovation.
8 — If you don't know a word"It is a kind of…" / "The opposite of…" / "Something like…" / "I cannot remember the word, but it means…"
9 — Never say only "yes" or "no"Always add a reason: "Yes, I agree because…" / "No, I don't think so because…"
10 — Don't translate word for wordThink in English, not in French. Use simple words you know.
11 — Read numbers aloud40% → "forty per cent" / 2024 → "twenty twenty-four" / 3 million → "three million".
12 — Conclude properly"To conclude…" / "In conclusion…" / "To sum up…" + one opening question.
